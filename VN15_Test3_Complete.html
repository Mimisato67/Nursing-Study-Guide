🩺 Nursing Process — Ch. 7
1
List the steps of the Nursing Process and explain the purpose of each step.
▼

The nursing process is a cyclical, critical thinking framework with five overlapping steps used to deliver client-centered care.

StepPurpose
1. AssessmentCollect subjective & objective data about the client's health status
2. Analysis/DiagnosisIdentify patterns, compare to standards, reach nursing judgments
3. PlanningSet priorities, establish measurable outcomes, select interventions
4. ImplementationCarry out the plan; delegate, supervise, and document care
5. EvaluationDetermine if goals were met; modify plan as needed
💡 Memory AidADPIE — Assessment · Diagnosis · Planning · Implementation · Evaluation

Clinical ReasoningThe thought process nurses use to understand a client's clinical situation
Clinical JudgmentThe conclusion nurses reach about a client's needs in a specific situation

💡 FlowCritical thinking → Clinical reasoning → Clinical judgment → Clinical decision-making

14
Describe the steps for assessing a client's ability to safely ambulate.
▼
  1. Review chart — diagnoses, medications, fall history, activity orders
  2. Assess baseline strength, ROM, and pain level
  3. Measure orthostatic blood pressure (lying → sitting → standing)
  4. Have client dangle at bedside — assess for dizziness before standing
  5. Assist client to stand — support at waist, assess stability
  6. Observe gait — balance, stride, shuffling, unsteadiness
  7. Determine if assistive device is needed (cane, walker, gait belt)
  8. Document findings — distance walked, tolerance, abnormal findings
⚠️ Safety FirstAlways apply a gait belt before ambulating. Have a second nurse if fall risk is high. Bed in low position.
15
Identify four principles of proper body mechanics and explain why they are important.
▼

Ch. 40: Body mechanics involve coordination between the musculoskeletal and nervous systems using alignment, balance, gravity, and friction.

PrincipleWhy Important
Wide base of support — feet shoulder-width apartIncreases stability; prevents falls during transfers
Low center of gravity — bend at knees, not waistReduces lumbar spine strain and back injury
Keep load close to bodyDecreases lever arm force on muscles and spine
Use large muscle groups — legs not backLeg muscles are stronger; prevents nurse injury

17
Explain how to safely use a cane during ambulation and when climbing stairs.
▼
Cane Instructions — Ch. 40
  • Keep cane on the stronger side of the body
  • Maintain two points of support on the ground at all times
  • Support body weight on both legs
  • Move cane forward 15–25 cm (6–10 in)
  • Move the weaker leg forward toward the cane
  • Advance the stronger leg past the cane
Stair Climbing
💡 "Up with the Good, Down with the Bad"⬆️ Going UP → Strong leg goes FIRST
⬇️ Going DOWN → Weak leg + cane go FIRST
18
How can nurses promote mobility, independence, and safety for clients with mobility limitations?
▼
Prevent Complications (Ch. 40)
  • Reposition every 1–2 hours to prevent pressure injuries
  • Use corrective devices (pillows, trochanter rolls, splints, foot boots)
  • Active or passive ROM 2–3 times/day
  • Use SCDs and elastic stockings for DVT prevention
  • Maintain fluid intake of at least 2,000 mL/day
Promote Independence
  • Use gait belts, walkers, canes, or crutches as needed
  • Refer to physical therapy for decreased mobility
  • Encourage active participation in ADLs
  • Cluster care to promote proper sleep-wake cycle
Psychosocial Support
  • Maintain orientation (clock, calendar, daily schedule)
  • Provide stimuli (books, TV, crafts, radio)
  • Encourage family visits and social interaction
🌿 Key PrincipleThe risk of complications increases with the degree AND length of immobilization — early mobilization is always the goal.
