Wound Care

Hydrofiber dressings are best used for:
Wounds with moderate-to-heavy exudate — the fiber absorbs fluid and gels to keep the bed moist.
Hydrogel dressings are primarily used for:
Dry wounds or wounds with minimal exudate and necrotic tissue — rehydrates and promotes autolytic debridement.
Hydrocolloid dressings should NOT be used when:
The wound has moderate-to-heavy drainage, is infected, is deep/necrotic, or has exposed muscle, tendon, or bone.
Polymeric membrane dressings are most appropriate when:
Exudate is mild-to-moderate — great for skin tears, partial-thickness wounds, and donor sites.

Pressure Injuries

List at least six risk factors for pressure injuries.
Immobility/inactivity, malnutrition, incontinence, peripheral neuropathy, vascular disease, hypoperfusion (also: diaphoresis, sedation, edema, dehydration, advanced age).
Describe age-related skin changes that increase pressure injury risk.
↓ elasticity, ↓ skin hydration, ↓ tissue regeneration, ↓ blood supply, and a thinner subcutaneous layer.
Stage 1
Intact skin, nonblanchable redness; may feel warmer or cooler than surrounding tissue.
Stage 2
Partial-thickness skin loss with exposed dermis; pink/red wound bed, no slough; intact or ruptured blister.
Stage 3
Full-thickness skin loss; visible adipose tissue, granulation tissue, rolled edges may be present; no exposed muscle, tendon, or bone.
Stage 4
Full-thickness skin AND tissue loss with exposed muscle, tendon, cartilage, or bone; tunneling/undermining common.

Skin & Integument

Functions of keratinocytes:
Produce keratin, which protects skin from water loss, bacteria, and injury.
Functions of melanocytes:
Produce melanin — the pigment of our skin, protecting it from UV radiation.
Functions of Merkel cells:
Sensory receptors for light touch, connected to nerve endings.
Functions of Langerhans cells:
Immune surveillance in the skin.

Mobility & Body Mechanics

List five principles of proper body mechanics.
1) Keep a wide base 2) Bend knees, not waist 3) Keep load close to body 4) Face the direction of movement 5) Keep back straight.
Describe the correct order for a mobility assessment.
1) Assess ROM in bed 2) Dangle at edge of bed 3) Stand client 4) Ambulate short distance.
Purpose of passive range of motion.
Maintains joint flexibility and prevents contractures in clients who can't move independently.
Correct use of a cane during ambulation and stairs.
Hold cane on the unaffected side; cane & weak leg move together. "Up with the good, down with the bad" — strong leg first going up, cane + weak leg first going down.

Urinary Elimination

Complications of urinary retention to the skin:
Dermatitis, skin breakdown, increased risk of pressure injury, and increased infection risk.
How can skin breakdown be prevented in incontinent clients?
Scheduled toileting, daily pericare, barrier cream, keep skin clean and dry, avoid harsh soaps/scrubbing.

Respiratory

What is COPD?
Chronic Obstructive Pulmonary Disease — progressive, largely irreversible airflow limitation (chronic bronchitis + emphysema).
Expected findings in COPD.
Dyspnea, chronic productive cough, wheezing, barrel chest, prolonged expiration, accessory muscle use, clubbing of fingers, hypoxemia & chronic hypercapnia.
Explain purse-lipped breathing.
Inhale through nose, exhale slowly through pursed lips — prolongs exhalation and reduces air trapping (key for COPD).
Important nursing considerations for a nonrebreather mask.
Assess for secure mask seal & skin breakdown; use the right mask size; eating/drinking/talking impaired; use cautiously with high aspiration risk.

Infection & Wounds

List signs of wound infection.
Purulent drainage, pain, redness & edema, fever & chills, odor, increased pulse & RR, ↑ WBC count.
Assessment findings that require provider notification.
↑ size/depth of wound, ↑ drainage, dehiscence or evisceration, signs of systemic sepsis.
Interventions that reduce wound infection risk.
Hand hygiene & aseptic technique during dressing changes, adequate hydration & nutrition, appropriate wound cleansing/irrigation, change dressing when soiled, antibiotics guided by C&S.
