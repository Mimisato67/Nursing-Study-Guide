🌿 Complete Study Guide

1. Cells pg. Introduction

The basic unit of biological organization of the human body — smallest living units capable of carrying out life functions.

2. Cytoplasm pg. 42

Mainly composed of water (70–90%), plus proteins, lipids, carbohydrates, and minerals.

3. Colloidal Solution pg. 42

A mixture where large protein molecules are suspended (not dissolved) in water — gives cytoplasm its gel-like consistency. Proteins too large to dissolve but too small to settle out.

4. Compounds That Don't Dissolve in Water pg. 43

Lipids (fats) — hydrophobic, do not dissolve in water.

5. Five Mineral Salts pg. 43

  • Sodium (Na)
  • Potassium (K)
  • Calcium (Ca)
  • Magnesium (Mg)
  • Phosphorus (P)

6. Chromosomes — Count pg. 44

46 total chromosomes (23 pairs) — 23 from each parent.

7. Chromosomes — Composition pg. 44

Made of DNA and proteins called histones.

8. Chromosome Duplication pg. 44

Copies are called sister chromatids, joined at the centromere.

9. Chromatin (Non-dividing Chromosomes) pg. 44

When not dividing, chromosomes are called chromatin — loosely coiled DNA + histone proteins.

10. Mitochondria & ATP pg. 45

Organelle: Mitochondria — converts chemical energy (food) into usable energy.

Energy produced: ATP (Adenosine Triphosphate)

11. Mitochondrial Folds (Cristae) pg. 45

Folds = cristae. More folds = more surface area = more ATP produced. High-energy cells (muscle, liver) have more cristae.

12. Transcription pg. 46

DNA is copied into mRNA (messenger RNA). Takes place in the nucleus. mRNA carries the genetic code to ribosomes for protein synthesis.

13. Cilia — Location & Purpose pg. 47

Found in: respiratory tract (trachea, bronchi) and fallopian tubes.

Purpose: move mucus, debris, and eggs.

14. Epithelial Cells pg. 55

Very little intercellular material. Cells are tightly packed, forming continuous sheets covering body surfaces.

15. Epithelial Cell Anchoring pg. 55

Anchored to connective tissue by a basement membrane. Connected to each other by desmosomes.

16. Bladder Tissue pg. 57

Transitional epithelium — can stretch and recoil as the bladder fills and empties.

17. Glands — Tissue Type pg. 58

All glands are made of epithelial tissue.

18. Endocrine vs Exocrine Glands pg. 58

🔴 Endocrine

Secrete hormones → bloodstream (NO ducts)

Ex: thyroid, adrenal glands

🔵 Exocrine

Secrete through ducts → to surface

Ex: sweat, salivary glands

19. Circulatory System Lining pg. 59

Endothelium — simple squamous epithelial tissue lining the heart, blood vessels, and lymphatic vessels.

20. Thoracic Cavity Lining pg. 59

Pleura — serous membrane. Parietal pleura = chest wall. Visceral pleura = lung surface.

21. Matrix pg. 60

Non-living intercellular material in connective tissue. Found in bone, cartilage, blood. Can be solid, semi-solid, gel-like, or liquid.

22. Mast Cell Substances pg. 61

Histamine

Vasodilation + increased capillary permeability (inflammation/allergy)

Heparin

Prevents blood clotting in area of inflammation

23. Adipose Tissue Functions pg. 62

  • Energy storage (stores fat as reserve fuel)
  • Thermal insulation (reduces heat loss)
  • Mechanical protection (cushions & protects organs from trauma)

24. Muscle → Bone Attachment pg. 63

Tendons — dense regular connective tissue.

25. Lacunae pg. 63

Small cavities/spaces housing cells. In bone → contain osteocytes. In cartilage → contain chondrocytes.

26. Blood — Tissue Type & Composition pg. 64

Type: Connective tissue (liquid matrix).

Composed of: Plasma (55%), RBCs, WBCs, Platelets.

27. Synovial Membranes pg. 65

Connective tissue. Lines synovial joints (knees, shoulders, hips). Secretes synovial fluid for lubrication.

28. Three Types of Muscle Tissue pg. 66

TypeLocationControl
SkeletalAttached to bonesVoluntary
SmoothOrgan wallsInvoluntary
CardiacHeart onlyInvoluntary

29. Smooth Muscle Locations pg. 67

  • Stomach, intestines, bladder, uterus
  • Blood vessel walls
  • Airways, eyes (iris)

30. Peristalsis pg. 67

Wave-like contractions propelling food through the digestive tract. Muscle involved: smooth muscle.

31. Cardiac Muscle — Unique Features pg. 68

  • Involuntary — cannot be consciously controlled
  • Has intercalated discs for electrical conduction
  • Autorhythmic — generates its own rhythm
  • Never fatigues under normal conditions
  • Found only in the heart

32. Nervous Tissue Locations pg. 69

Found in: brain, spinal cord, peripheral nerves. Made of neurons and neuroglia.
