2. Pulse

Normal Adult Range: 60–100 beats/min at rest

What to Document:

  • Rate (beats per minute)
  • Rhythm (regular or irregular)
  • Strength/amplitude (bounding, strong, weak, thready)
  • Equality (compare bilateral pulses)

Causes of Higher Pulse (Tachycardia >100):

  • Fever, medications, acute pain
  • Hyperthyroidism, anemia, hypoxemia
  • Stress/anxiety/fear
  • Hypovolemia, shock, heart failure, hemorrhage
  • Changing positions

Causes of Lower Pulse (Bradycardia <60):

  • Long-term physical fitness/athletes
  • Hypothermia, medications
  • Chronic severe pain, relaxation

Pulse Deficit:

Beat at apical site with NO pulsation at radial pulse. Apical pulse > peripheral pulse. Indicates cardiac inefficiency.

Apical Pulse Location:

Left midclavicular line at the 5th intercostal space (ICS)

Irregular Heartbeat Findings:

  • Palpitations (>100 beats/min) or slow HR (<60 beats/min)
  • Premature, late, or missed heartbeats
  • Irregular intervals between impulses

3. Blood Pressure

Reflects force the blood exerts against the walls of arteries during cardiac muscle contraction (systole) and relaxation (diastole).

Normal: <120/80 mmHg
ConditionReadingAction
HypertensionConsistently >120/80 mmHg (commonly above 140/90)Seek help or treat if associated with other issues
HypotensionBelow 90/60 mmHgAssess for cause, notify provider

How to Take Blood Pressure:

  • Patient has back support, uncrossed feet
  • Check for the right cuff size
  • Apply cuff 2 inches above elbow
  • Make sure arm is elevated and rested at heart level

Causes of Higher BP:

  • Older adults, stress, ethnicity, menopause
  • Adolescence in males, medications, obesity
  • Family history, high sodium intake

Causes of Lower BP:

  • Exercise, circadian rhythm
  • Infants have lower BP
Postural/Orthostatic Hypotension: BP drops suddenly when changing positions. Both terms mean the same thing — just different names.

4. Temperature

Reflects body's internal and external temperature; the unit/location being measured matters.

Normal Adult: 97.6–99.6°F (36.4–37.6°C) oral
Fever in Adult: 100.4°F (38°C) or higher — keep patient hydrated, encourage rest, make sure patient is warm/comfortable

Methods of Taking Temperature:

RouteNotes
Forehead (temporal artery)Non-invasive, quick
OralMost common; wait 30 min after eating/drinking
RectalMost accurate; 1° higher than oral
Armpit (axillary)Least accurate; 1° lower than oral
Ear (tympanic)Quick, good for children

Alternative methods used when body parts are not accessible or depending on age, health status, and current status.

Causes of Higher Temperature:

  • Dehydration, heat exhaustion, fever
  • Vital organ damage, heat stroke

Causes of Lower Temperature:

  • Nervous system depression, metabolism issues
  • Environment, cardiovascular and respiratory distress

5. Cardiovascular Assessment

Mix of both objective and subjective data — patient history, physical measurements, and diagnostic results.

Data Collected:

  • Heart rate and rhythm
  • Blood pressure
  • Peripheral pulses
  • Skin color, temperature, capillary refill
  • Edema assessment
  • Heart sounds (S1, S2)
  • Patient history and symptoms

6. Oxygen Saturation (SpO2)

Normal Range: 95–100%
Low O2 Sat: Below 92% — requires immediate intervention

How to Obtain:

Place oximeter probe on vascular area — commonly fingers, toes, or earlobe — for 10–30 seconds.

Causes of Low O2 Sat:

  • Reading drops below 92%
  • Lung conditions (pneumonia, COPD)
  • Heart issues
  • Environmental factors
  • Physical signs: shortness of breath, chest pain

Nursing Actions for Low O2 Sat:

  • Verify the reading first
  • Monitor/assess symptoms: SOB, cyanosis, confusion
  • Restore oxygenation — sit patient up
  • Provide supplemental oxygen (follow protocol)
  • Notify provider

7. Teeth and Oral Care

  • Important for digestion and peristalsis
  • Poor oral hygiene can increase risk of aspiration pneumonia
  • Can worsen systematic conditions like diabetes
  • Allows patient to feel clean and comfortable
  • Assess oral cavity regularly
  • Provide oral care at least twice daily
