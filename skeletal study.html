— Chapter Review —
1
What makes up the skeletal system? pg.138
The skeletal system includes all the bones of the body and their associated cartilage, tendons, and ligaments.
2
What is the function of osteoclasts? pg.142
Osteoclasts are responsible for the reabsorption of bone — large cells that break down bone tissue and release stored calcium into the bloodstream.
3
How many types of ossification are there, and what does ossification mean? pg.142–143
Ossification = the formation of bone.
Intramembranous: dense connective tissue membranes are replaced by deposits of inorganic calcium salts to form bone.
Endochondral: process in which cartilage is the environment in which bone cells develop (endo = inside, chondro = cartilage).
4
What are lacunae and what do they contain? pg.144
Lacunae are found between 2 lamellae (rings); they contain an osteocyte suspended in tissue fluid.
5
Define fracture. pg.146
A fracture is the breaking of bone or associated cartilage, usually accompanied by injury to soft tissues like muscle or connective tissue.
6
Explain diaphysis. pg.147
The diaphysis (shaft) is present in the structure of long bones and consists mainly of compact bone.
7
Explain flat bones. pg.148
Flat bones are thin bones found wherever there's a need for extensive muscle attachment or protection of soft/vital parts. Usually curved, they consist of 2 flat plates of compact bone enclosing a layer of spongy bone.
Ex: sternum, ribs, scapula, parts of pelvic bone & some skull bones
8
Where is the frontal bone? pg.150
A single bone that forms the forehead, the roof of the nasal cavity, and the orbits (bony sockets containing the eyes) — the anterior side of the cranium.
9
What is the opening in the occipital bone, and what's significant about it? pg.151
The foramen magnum — it connects the brain with the spinal cord.
10
Explain the temporal bone. pg.151
The temporal bones help form the lower sides and base of the cranium. Each one encloses an ear and bears a fossa for articulation with the mandible. Irregular shape; contains 4 parts: squamous, petrous, mastoid, and tympanic.
11
Explain the sphenoid bone. pg.151
The sphenoid bone forms the anterior portion of the base of the cranium. Looks like a butterfly; acts as an anchor binding all the cranial bones together. Present inside the eye socket.
12
What bones form the bridge of the nose? pg.153
Nasal bones.
13
What is the strongest bone of the jaw? pg.152
The mandible.
14
What are vertebrae? pg.156
26 irregular bones that make up our spinal column.
15
What is another name for the sternum? pg.160
Breastbone.
16
What are true ribs?
The upper 7 pairs that articulate directly with the sternum.
17a
Define: Humerus
The largest and longest bone of the upper arm. Has a round head and is joined to the rest of the bone by its anatomic neck.
17b
Define: Femur
The thigh bone — largest and heaviest bone of the body.
17c
Define: Patella
The kneecap — largest of the sesamoid bones.
17d
Define: Tarsal
Bones of the ankles.
— Medical Terminology —
1
Describe kyphosis. kyph/o = bent/hump
Abnormal increase in the outward curvature of the thoracic spine, as viewed from the side.
2
Describe lordosis.
Abnormal increase in the forward curvature of the lumbar spine. Also known as swayback.
3
Describe the sacrum. sacr/o
A slightly curved, triangular-shaped bone near the base of the spine that forms the lower portion of the back.
4
Describe the location of the cervical spine.
Neck region — 1st set of 7 vertebrae, C1 through C7.
5
Describe the location of the thoracic spine.
T1 through T12 — second set of 12 vertebrae, in the thoracic cavity.
6
Describe the location of the lumbar spine.
Part of the back and sides between the ribs and pelvis. L1 through L5 — 3rd set of 5 vertebrae.
7
What is the other name for the coccyx?
Tailbone.
8
What does the prefix peri- describe?
Surrounding.
9
Word part: -malacia
Abnormal softening (e.g., osteomalacia).
10
Word part: -ago
Diseased condition (e.g., lumbago).
11
Word part: -penia
Deficiency (e.g., osteopenia).
12
Word part: -algia
Pain (e.g., myalgia).
13
Word part: -sclerosis
Abnormal hardening (e.g., arteriosclerosis).
14
Word part: scoli-
Curved (e.g., scoliosis).
15
Word part: lord-
Bent backward (e.g., lordosis / swayback).
16
Word part: ankyl-
Crooked, bent, or stiff (e.g., ankylosing spondylitis).
17
Word part: capsul-
Little box (e.g., joint capsule).
18
Word part: kyph-
Hump (e.g., kyphosis).
