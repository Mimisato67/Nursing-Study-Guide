Quick Reference — Simplified

3 Core Definitions

  • Anatomy — study of structure/morphology of body parts and how they're organized
  • Physiology — study of functions of body parts, what they do and how they do it
  • Pathology — study of diseases of the body

Directional Terms

Superior — uppermost/above
Inferior — lowermost/below
Anterior — towards the front
Posterior — towards the back
Cranial — towards the head
Medial — towards midline
Lateral — away from midline
Proximal — nearer point of attachment
Distal — further from point of attachment
Midsagittal — divides into 2 equal halves
Horizontal/Transverse — divides superior/inferior
Frontal/Coronal — divides anterior/posterior
Planes — imaginary flat geometric surfaces

Body Cavities

  • Diaphragm separates thoracic and abdominopelvic cavity
  • Heart → thoracic cavity (pericardial cavity)
  • Brain → cranial cavity
  • Abdominopelvic: kidneys, stomach, liver, gallbladder, small & large intestines, spleen, pancreas, ovaries/uterus

Organization of Life (smallest → largest)

Atoms→Molecules→Organelles→Cells→Tissues→Organs→Systems
  • Cells — smallest living units of biologic organization
  • Molecules — atoms combined through chemical bonds (water, sugar, amino acids)
  • Organelles — tiny structures performing specific cell functions
  • Tissues — cells performing similar functions
  • Organs — tissues serving a common function

4 Tissue Types

  • Epithelial — covers & protects inner/outer surfaces of organs
  • Connective — binds together & supports other tissues/organs
  • Muscle — elongated cells that generate movement by shortening/contracting
  • Nerve — transmits signals (implied from nervous system)

10 Body Systems

  • Integumentary — insulation, protection, regulation
  • Skeletal — protects vital parts, supports tissues, body movement
  • Muscular — movement, pushes food, blood flow, heart contraction
  • Nervous — controls/regulates body systems, interprets stimulus, controls senses
  • Endocrine — produces hormones that chemically regulate body functions
  • Cardiovascular — pumps and distributes blood
  • Lymphatic — drains excess fluid, absorbs fats, develops immunities
  • Respiratory — brings oxygen, eliminates carbon dioxide
  • Urinary — blood regulation, urine formation/elimination, homeostasis
  • Digestive — converts food to nutrients, eliminates waste

Homeostasis & Feedback

  • Homeostasis — maintenance of internal environment of the body
  • Positive feedback — responds by INCREASING and amplifying the change
    Ex: labor contractions continue intensifying until baby is born
  • Negative feedback — responds by REVISING disturbances to return to normal
    Ex: thermostat turns furnace off when temp exceeds 72°F

Medical Terminology

  • -itis — inflammation
  • -algia / DYNA — pain
  • -ectomy — surgical removal of
  • post- — after
  • micro- — small

Anterior vs Posterior

Anterior = A = Ahead (Front)
AAnterior = Ahead = Front of body
PPosterior = Posterior = Back (like your backPOSTerior 😂)
💡 Your "posterior" in everyday slang IS your backside — posterior = back!

Proximal vs Distal

ProXimal = X marks the origin
PProximal = closer to where it attaches (shoulder is proximal to elbow)
DDistal = Distant = further away (fingers are distal to elbow)
💡 Distal = Distant. The further it is, the more distant!

Positive vs Negative Feedback

+ = Push More, - = Pull Back
+Positive feedback = AMPLIFIES the change (labor contractions keep going)
-Negative feedback = CORRECTS the change (thermostat turns OFF)
💡 Positive = keeps PUSHING in same direction. Negative = PULLS back to normal.
