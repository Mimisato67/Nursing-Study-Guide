Quick Reference — Simplified

Nurse Practice Act (NPA)

  • Regulates nursing in each state/territory
  • Sets scope & standards of practice
  • Establishes licensure requirements
  • Identifies nursing titles; Board of Nursing enforces it

QSEN — 6 Competencies

Patient-Centered Care Teamwork EBP Quality Improvement Safety Informatics

Prepares nurses to improve healthcare quality & client outcomes.

State Board of Nursing

  • Enforces NPA rules
  • Regulates/monitors nursing practice
  • Issues & renews licenses
  • Oversees violations & disciplinary actions

NLN — 4 Core Competencies

Caring Integrity Diversity Excellence

Supports nursing education, faculty development & professional growth.

LVN Scope of Practice

  • Medication administration (per state regs)
  • Basic nursing care, documentation
  • Reinforce teaching
  • Works under RN/physician supervision
  • Data gathering & assessment contributions

Scope determined by the NPA

Standards of Practice — Examples

Data Collection Nursing Diagnosis Planning Outcome ID Implementation Evaluation Health Teaching Coordination of Care

QSEN Competency Breakdown

  • Patient-Centered Care — respect client preferences & needs
  • Teamwork & Collaboration — work with interdisciplinary team
  • EBP — use current research in care decisions
  • Quality Improvement — improve outcomes & systems
  • Safety — reduce risk of harm to client & staff
  • Informatics — use tech to communicate & manage info safely

Licensure Requirements

Initial: Graduate approved program → Pass NCLEX-PN → Disclose substance history → Complete state application

Renewal: Renewal application → Continuing education → Pay fees → Comply with Board requirements

Code of Ethics — Purpose

  • Guides professional nursing behavior
  • Protects client rights
  • Promotes professionalism & accountability
  • Provides ethical standards for decision-making
  • Ensures safe & ethical care

7 Ethical Principles

  • Fidelity — keep promises to client
  • Beneficence — do good, act in client's best interest
  • Nonmaleficence — do no harm
  • Autonomy — respect client's right to decide
  • Justice — treat all fairly, no bias
  • Veracity — tell the truth
  • Integrity — honesty & strong moral principles

Legal Terms

  • Libel — written false statements damaging reputation
  • Defamation — communication of false statements damaging reputation
  • Tort — civil wrong against person/property
  • Malpractice — failure to follow accepted standard of care
  • Good Samaritan Law — protects providers giving emergency care outside workplace

TeamSTEPPS Components

  • Team Structure — identify members & roles
  • Leadership — coordinate activities & resources
  • Situation Monitoring — maintain awareness of environment & client status
  • Mutual Support — provide assistance & feedback
  • Communication — structured communication to improve safety

Interprofessional Team Roles

  • Nurse — coordinates care & advocates for patients
  • Physician/Provider — diagnoses & manages treatment
  • Pharmacist — manages medications
  • Respiratory Therapist — respiratory care
  • PT — mobility & function
  • OT — daily living skills
  • Social Worker — psychosocial & financial needs
  • Dietician — nutritional management

Professional vs Unprofessional

Professional: Integrity, responsibility, confidentiality, scope of practice, accountability, continuing ed, join professional orgs

Unprofessional: Breach of confidentiality, falsifying records, practicing outside scope, leaving computer unattended while logged in, failing standards of care

Key Communication Tools

I-SBAR-R: Introduction → Situation → Background → Assessment → Recommendation → Read Back

Breach of Confidentiality: Unauthorized disclosure of private client info (e.g., visible charts, discussing in public)

Occurrence Report: Documents errors/falls/injuries — NOT part of medical record. Purpose: improve safety, identify risks, support QI.

Handoff Report Includes

Client ID Current Condition Diagnosis Pertinent History Pending Tests Assessment Findings Treatments & Meds Safety Concerns Ongoing Care Recs

Leadership Models

  • Situational — adapts style to situation, flexible
  • Laissez-Faire — minimal direction, high autonomy
  • Transformational — inspires staff, shared vision, growth
  • Transactional — rewards/consequences, performance-focused
  • Bureaucratic — rule-based, strictly enforces policies
